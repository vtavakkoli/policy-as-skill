# Synthetic EU AI Act Inspired Policy Snippets

## high-risk-ai
AI systems used for eligibility, access to public services, education, employment, or law-enforcement support are treated as high-risk and require documented risk management, data governance, logging, transparency, and human oversight.

## prohibited-use
AI systems must not manipulate vulnerable people, infer sensitive traits without a lawful basis, or make fully automated adverse decisions about citizens without contestability and meaningful human review.

## transparency
Users and affected people should receive clear notice when AI materially supports a decision. The notice should explain purpose, limitations, data categories, and how to request review.

## human-review
High-impact or adverse recommendations require a trained human reviewer before implementation. The reviewer must be able to inspect evidence, override the system, and record justification.
